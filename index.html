Overview

UniversalPose employs a single shared encoder for multiple sensing modalities (WiFi, mmWave, LiDAR, Depth, acoustic), enabling joint training with unpaired asynchronous datasets and seamless multi-modal fusion with synchronized paired data.

Model Architecture

Pipeline and Universal Encoder. All input signals are mapped into a shared representation space using modality-specific tokenizers and positional encoders, then processed by a single Universal Encoder with local attention and Modality Conditional Adaptation (MoCA) for adaptive modality alignment.

Experimental Results

Baseline Comparison

Accuracy comparison against per-modality expert models; a single jointly-trained UniversalPose matches or surpasses specialized baselines across all six modalities.

Fusion Comparison

Multi-modal fusion results compared with X-Fi; UniversalPose outperforms the state-of-the-art across most modality combinations without requiring frozen modality-specific encoders.

Ablation Study

Ablation on the Universal Encoder (vs. OMNIVORE-style global attention), MoCA, and joint training, evaluated at both small and large data scales.

MoCA Feature Analysis

Intermediate feature visualization. PCA of features from block 3 across modalities (color-coded); MoCA substantially reduces modality-specific clustering, especially under limited training data.

Ours (Full)

With MoCA on full training data, features from all modalities are well-aligned in a shared representation space, enabling effective knowledge transfer.

w/o MoCA (Full)

Without MoCA, some clustering by modality emerges, indicating the model learns more modality-specific rather than shared features.

Ours (Small)

Even with limited training data, MoCA maintains good feature alignment across modalities, demonstrating its regularization benefits.

w/o MoCA (Small)

Without MoCA and with limited data, strong modality clustering occurs, leading to poor generalization and knowledge transfer.
